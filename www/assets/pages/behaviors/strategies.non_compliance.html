Hold kids accountable to build responsibility and self-concept. Ask them questions to generate thinking. This sends a powerful message that their noncompliance is not the teacher's problem, and that there is a consequence the student will eventually face. Asking them questions prompts them to think through their problems and reflect on their behavior. Questions include:

  • What will you do about this problem?
  • What would you like to see happen?
  • How do you suppose this will work out?
  • On a scale of 1-5, how good of a decision do you think this is?
  • What do you think I think about that?
  • Would you like to hear what other kids have tried?
  • Some kids do A, some B. What do you think will work best for you?
