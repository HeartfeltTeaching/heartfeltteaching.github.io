• Develop positive relationships
  • Going brain dead will prevent arguments in the classroom
  • Heavy doses of empathy allows you to remain the "good guy" while allowing the student's poor decision to be the "bad guy."
  • Consequence for early-stage misbehavior delivered with empathy will give the student information about the offense and is more likely to be corrected.
  • Providing choices not only helps children think for themselves, it may also motivates them. "You may_____," or "I will_____", or "Feel free to______."
  • Enforceable statement: Start by describing what you are willing to do or allow. Don't tell kids how to run their lives. Instead tell them how you are going to run yours. "I will be glad to listen when your voice sounds as calm as mine." "I will start when everyone is seated."
  • Remember: Students will always come to need at least the same number of warnings and reminders as they were given.
  • Delayed Consequences: When you are unsure how to proceed with an aggressive student or you may be too angry to think, delay the consequence. For example, "I am going to have to do something about this." Not now, later. In the meantime, try not to worry about it."
