• Step 1: Go "Brain Dead"- The less our lips move, the more effective we become. Resisting the urge to counter with a comeback, makes it easier to avoid diving into pointless quarrels. Listening, not arguing is the best way to shift a mindset.
  • Step 2: Calmly repeat a Love and Logic One Liner- When we remember to calmly repeat the very same thing… regardless of what our students say… we starve them of argument fuel. Eventually, they will quit complaining. Repeat the same One Liner every time your student tries to argue. Below are examples of some favorite one liners:
    • "I care too much to argue."
    • "I know."
    • "What did I say?"
    • "Thank you for sharing." 
  • Use an Empathetic Statement - Empathy allows you to hold students accountable without losing their respect. Examples of empathetic statements:
    • "What a Bummer"
    • "I can’t imagine how bad that feels"
    • "I care too much to argue"
  • Encourage Thinking - Thinking prompts students to begin thinking about possible solutions and their likely consequences.
    • "What do you think I am thinking?"
    • "What would you like to see happen?"
    • "Would you like to hear what other students have tried?"
    • "Some students do A, some do B."
    • "What do you think will work best for you?"
