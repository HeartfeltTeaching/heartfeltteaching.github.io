• Guide the conversation by asking questions for example,
    • "What do you think I just saw?"
    • "Can you explain what I just saw?"

Remember: Questions generate thinking!

  • Assist students problem solve and generate questions for example,
    • "What do you think you should do?"
    • "How can I help?"
    • "Would you like to hear what other kids have tried?"

Remember: The person who does most thinking about a problem will learn the most from the problem.

  • If the problem can't be solved or is chosen not to, you will do something for example,
    • "I will try to find the best solution or best consequence based on what I know about the situation and the problem. All people are unique and all problems are unique, so I won't be telling anyone in advance what's going to happen if they can't or won't solve a problem they create. You just need to know that I'm going to do something."
